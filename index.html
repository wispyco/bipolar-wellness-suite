Research Foundation

Every tool in this suite is grounded in peer-reviewed clinical research and validated assessment instruments.

Life Chart Method

Developed by the NIMH, the Life Chart Method is a validated prospective daily mood monitoring tool used extensively in bipolar disorder research and clinical practice since the 1990s.

Evidence-Based Therapies

Tools incorporate principles from CBT (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy), IPSRT (Interpersonal and Social Rhythm Therapy), and functional remediation — all with demonstrated efficacy for bipolar disorder.

Self-Management Science

Research consistently shows that structured self-monitoring and psychoeducation improve outcomes in bipolar disorder, reducing relapse rates and improving quality of life when used alongside clinical care.

This suite is for educational and self-management purposes. It is not a substitute for professional medical care. If you are in crisis, please contact your healthcare provider or call the 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline.
